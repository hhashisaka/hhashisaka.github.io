About me

Background

I'm a research analyst with a deep interest in health and wellbeing ecosystems — from the bodily to the organizational. I approach problems by zooming out to the 50,000-foot view first, then zeroing in with precision to find what actually matters.

My work sits at the intersection of population health, data science, and policy — defining and maintaining data pipelines, building dashboards for epidemiological teams, and translating technical findings into language that drives decisions for public-sector partners.

I believe good analysis isn't just about the numbers. It's about understanding the human experience behind the data and shaping insights that connect with the people who need them most.
